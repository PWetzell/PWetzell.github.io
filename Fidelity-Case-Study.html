The Problem

A dashboard built for passive summaries, not the intraday decisions traders actually needed to make.

Fidelity's Global Cash dashboard lacked real-time data clarity and the density required to support intraday liquidity decisions. Traders were working around the tool rather than through it — relying on manual validation, fragmented workflows, and inconsistent data presentation across Active Trader system components.

The core tension

The existing system couldn't support trading-grade decision velocity. High-stakes data needed to be trusted instantly — not cross-referenced manually.

The platform's layout hierarchy didn't reflect how traders actually prioritized information. Timestamp trust, data freshness indicators, and multi-currency visibility were either absent or buried — adding friction at exactly the wrong moment.

My Role

Embedded Senior UX Designer within Fidelity's Center of Excellence, leading the full redesign of Global Cash.

What I owned
End-to-end redesign of Global Cash into a trading-grade workspace — information architecture, layout systems, interaction patterns, and implementation-ready UX specs.
How I worked
Cross-functional collaboration with traders, cash managers, product, and engineering to validate workflows, align on feasibility, and deliver specs adopted across the Fixed Income ecosystem.
The constraints
Governed layout, toolbar, and streaming patterns that had to remain consistent across Active Trader system components — no isolated solutions.
Process

Six phases from discovery to delivery — each one building the foundation for the next.

Partnered with traders, product, engineering, and the CoE to validate real workflows, improve data density, and establish governed patterns that support trading-grade clarity across Global Cash.

01
Discovery & Inquiry
Conducted working sessions with traders, cash managers, and CoE stakeholders to uncover workflow friction, timestamp trust issues, and density constraints. Identified gaps in hierarchy, density, and timestamp trust.
02
System Evaluation
Assessed the Kx5 and Active Trader components — clarifying where each system needed to support trading-grade decisions. Documented handoff scenarios and escalation paths.
03
Hybrid Model Definition
Created temporary components to simulate tighter grids, cleaner named hierarchy, and consistent toolbar behavior. Validated patterns through iterative prototype reviews with traders.
04
Unified Layout Strategy
Established governed spacing, alignment, and structural rules across all modules to reduce cognitive load and improve navigation speed. Defined rate-based dashboard layouts for high-volume data tasks.
05
Data Freshness & Trust Patterns
Designed persistent timestamp browsing, refresh indicators, and explicit data-age signals to eliminate manual cross-referencing and surface stale values immediately.
06
Design-to-Dev Governance
Developed implementation-ready interaction patterns and density guidelines adopted across the Fixed Income CoE. Delivered cross-team design alignment documentation for ongoing iteration.
Interaction patterns

Built for high-volume tasks

Rate-based layouts, governed toolbar systems, and persistent navigation designed to support rapid context switching across multi-currency portfolios without losing state.

Cross-team alignment

One spec, three teams

Implementation-ready UX specs adopted by product and engineering across the Fixed Income ecosystem — reducing back-and-forth and accelerating delivery velocity.

Outcomes

A platform traders could trust — and a model the organization could build on.

Faster liquidity decisions through cleaner streaming and refined data presentation that reduced cognitive load at decision points.
Reduced manual validation through persistent data-freshness indicators — traders no longer needed to cross-reference timestamps externally.
Improved data density and numeric standards — higher grids and cleaner numeric hierarchy made high-volume scanning significantly faster.
Increased trader confidence through consistent, trust-reinforcing support access and data presentation standards applied across all modules.
Improved decision clarity through cleaner data presentation and a layout hierarchy aligned to how traders prioritize information under time pressure.
Cross-team alignment via a hybrid Kx5 and Active Trader model — recognized by CoE as a foundation for future Fixed Income workspace modernization.

What I'd do differently

Start the system evaluation phase earlier and map trader mental models explicitly before touching layouts. The biggest friction points weren't visual — they were workflow sequencing issues that only surfaced once we had prototypes in front of active traders. Earlier exposure to real decision scenarios would have sharpened the information hierarchy faster.
